Frequently Asked Questions

What is gps tracking?

GPS tracking is a technology that uses satellites to determine the location of automobiles, humans, or gadgets in real-time, and monitor their movement on a map.

How do GPS tracking devices work?

GPS monitoring devices use satellite signals to pinpoint their location via trilateration and ship this data to a primary server through mobile, satellite, or internet connections, enabling actual-time location tracking on a virtual map.

What hardware is needed for GPS?

GE-AMEco and GE-M2M+ are the hardware that can be used to track the real-time location of your asset. Our GPS tracking system uses advanced 2G and 4G modems for real-time monitoring of vehicles and assets. It features auto APN, domain-to-IP mapping, adaptive communication, easy management tools, automation, alerts, and detailed reporting for efficient tracking and management.

Is GPS hardware or software?

GPS (Global Positioning System) is a combination of hardware and software, with hardware components such as GPS receivers, antennas, and sensors, and GPS firmware, applications, mapping, and tracking algorithms, and data analysis tools with functional software elements including integrated reception of satellite signals, processing of data, and provision of location information and tracking capabilities.
